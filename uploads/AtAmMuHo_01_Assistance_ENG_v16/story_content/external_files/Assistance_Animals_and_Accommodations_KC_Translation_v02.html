Under federal laws, which of the following does NOT fit the definition of an assistance animal?

  1. A dog who cheers up hospital patients and lives with a resident who doesn’t have a disability
  2. A guide dog for a blind resident
  3. A cat that alleviates a resident’s depression symptoms
  4. A bird that alleviates the symptoms of a resident’s anxiety disorder

A resident requests a reasonable accommodation for a cat that provides them with emotional support related to a documented disability. A coworker says, “We have to deny this request because only dogs can be service animals.” Which of the following is the best response to your coworker?

  1. You’re right. We don’t have to allow cats.
  2. You’re right. We only have to make accommodations for service animals, not emotional support animals.
  3. That’s not right. Cats can still be assistance animals; we need to consider the request.

Which of the following is one of the criteria for a person to be protected by the federal law regarding assistance animals?

  1. The person must be at least 18 years of age.
  2. The person must have an animal with documented training.
  3. The person must have a disability.
  4. The person must be requesting a dog.

James is a prospective resident who uses a guide dog. It’s clear that the dog is trained for this task and that James requires it. He requests an exception to your no-pets policy as a reasonable accommodation. What should you ask James in relation to this request?

  1. Ask for a doctor’s note to verify James’s disability.
  2. Ask James how long he’s had the disability.
  3. Ask James to complete a reasonable accommodation request form.

Paulo, a resident, asks leasing consultant Sheila what kinds of animals he’s allowed to have in his apartment. When Sheila tells him that cats, dogs, and fish are permitted, he looks disappointed. “My therapist thinks that I could really use a bird,” he says. “Is there anything you can do?” Which of the following is Sheila’s best response?

  1. Sheila can’t do anything until Paulo specifically uses the words “assistance animal” and “accommodation.”
  2. Sheila can ask Paulo if he needs the bird as an accommodation for a disability.
  3. Sheila should ask Paulo for permission to call his therapist directly.

Which of the following is the best statement to include in a no pets-policy to help ensure that the policy isn’t seen as discriminatory?

  1. We comply with all federal, state, and local laws regarding assistance animals.
  2. Out of fairness to residents with allergies, we don’t allow animals in this community.
  3. We occasionally make exceptions for small, well-behaved emotional support dogs.
  4. If you have an animal, we can recommend some local pet-friendly communities.

Carlos tells his coworker, Walker, that he’s going to suggest that a resident get an assistance animal. “My mom struggles with the same kinds of things,” he says. “It really helped her.” What should Walker say to Carlos?

  1. “If you think it would help, I think that’s a great idea.”
  2. “I don’t think you should say anything. It’s not good to make assumptions about what a resident might need.”
